# User Interviews

## Summary

5 interviews conducted via Twitter DMs across engineering managers, CTOs, and solo founders who manage AI tool budgets for their teams. Interviews were informal (15–20 min).
---

## Interview 1

**Profile:** Engineering Manager, 8-person startup, Series A  
**Tools used:** Cursor Pro, GitHub Copilot Business, ChatGPT Team  
**Monthly AI spend:** ~$620/month

**Key quotes:**
- "I honestly don't know if we're on the right plans. I just approved what the devs asked for."
- "Our CFO asked me to justify the AI spend last quarter. I had no good answer."
- "I'd share something like this in our #eng-ops Slack channel immediately if it gave me a number I could defend."

**What they care about:**
- Justifying spend to non-technical stakeholders
- Not looking uninformed in front of their team
- Speed ,they don't want to research pricing themselves

**What would make them share it:**
- A specific dollar savings number they didn't expect
- Something they could paste into a Slack message or Google Doc

---

## Interview 2

**Profile:** Solo founder, pre-seed, 2 people  
**Tools used:** Cursor Pro, Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus  
**Monthly AI spend:** ~$240/month (paying for both himself and his co-founder)

**Key quotes:**
- "I'm definitely on too many plans. I just haven't had time to think about it."
- "I feel like I'm subscribed to everything and actually using nothing fully."
- "If it told me I could cut $80/month by switching one plan, I'd do it that day."

**What they care about:**
- Runway every dollar matters at this stage
- Not spending time on this problem wants instant clarity
- Feeling like they made a smart decision

**What would make them share it:**
- The "oh god" moment when they see what they're actually wasting
- Posting it on Twitter as a "built this, found this" moment

## Interview 3

**Profile:** CTO, 20-person startup, bootstrapped  
**Tools used:** GitHub Copilot Business (whole team), Cursor Teams (subset), OpenAI API  
**Monthly AI spend:** ~$1,800/month

**Key quotes:**
- "At this scale the waste is real but invisible. Nobody owns it."
- "We added Copilot for everyone but I bet half the team never opened it."
- "I'd want this to flag per-tool waste, not just give me a total."

**What they care about:**
- Per-tool breakdown, not just aggregate savings
- Seat utilization, paying for unused seats is the biggest issue
- Something they can share with their ops/finance person

**What would make them share it:**
- A shareable report URL they can send to their co-founder or CFO
- Seeing a specific tool called out ("you're wasting $380/month on unused Copilot seats")

## Interview 4

- Forwarding it to their manager as "found this, thought it was relevant"

## Interview 5

**Profile:** Founder, B2B SaaS, 12-person team  
**Tools used:** Cursor Pro (all devs), Claude Team, Windsurf (2 devs experimenting)  
**Monthly AI spend:** ~$950/month

**Key quotes:**
- "We're paying for Windsurf AND Cursor for some people. That's definitely redundant."
- "I added Claude Team when it launched and honestly can't remember why."
- "I would absolutely share this if the result surprised me."

**What they care about:**
- Eliminating redundant tool overlap
- Having a clear recommendation, not just data
- Something that stays updated as pricing changes

**What would make them share it:**
- A result that confirmed what they suspected but couldn't prove
- Sharing with their team so devs can self-audit

---

## Patterns Across Interviews

**Pain points:**
1. Nobody owns AI spend, it accumulates tool by tool, month by month
2. Seat waste is the #1 hidden cost (paying for seats people don't use)
3. Overlap between tools (Cursor + Windsurf, Claude + ChatGPT) is common and unexamined
4. Non-technical stakeholders (CFO, co-founder) are asking questions engineering can't answer well

**Share triggers:**
1. A specific, surprising dollar number ("I'm wasting $380/month")
2. A result they can forward to justify a decision
3. The "Spotify Wrapped" effect , part confession, part flex on Twitter/LinkedIn

**What they don't want:**
- To sign up before seeing results
- Vague recommendations ("consider optimizing your stack")
- A tool that feels like it's selling them something

---

## Implications for Product

- Per-seat math matters more than aggregate totals
- The output needs to be specific enough to screenshot and share
- Shareable report URL is a high-priority feature for virality
- "Seat utilization" input (how many seats are actually active) would unlock the most valuable recommendations
- The email capture after results is the right call — everyone confirmed they'd provide email for a PDF after seeing the audit
